S5-TTS: Low-Latency Streaming T5-based Text-to-Speech Synthesis with Minimal Lookahead

Abstract

Low-latency and natural text-to-speech (TTS) is crucial for interactive AI systems, but most TTS models require full sentences before synthesis, resulting in delayed responses. Existing incremental TTS (iTTS) methods can reduce latency but have yet to leverage the high naturalness and speaker diversity offered by LLM-based TTS. We propose S5-TTS, a streaming variant of T5-TTS that enables word-by-word synthesis with minimal lookahead. S5-TTS incorporates a lookahead-causal masking strategy for generating speech with high intelligibility and speaker similarity, an auxiliary attention module that facilitates mask construction and accelerates monotonic alignment learning, and utilizes an interleaved multi-source distillation approach to further restore naturalness and perceived quality under limited lookahead. Experiments demonstrate that S5-TTS achieves comparable naturalness and intelligibility to T5-TTS, with the capability to perform zero-shot speech synthesis for unseen speakers, while significantly reducing end-to-end speech response latency.
